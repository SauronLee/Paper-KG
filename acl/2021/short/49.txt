Happy Dance, Slow Clap: Using Reaction GIFs to Predict Induced Affect on Twitter | Boaz Shmueli | datasets with induced emotion labels are scarce but of utmost importance for many nlp tasks. we present a new , automated method for collecting texts along with their induced reaction labels. the method exploits the online use of reaction gifs , which capture complex affective states. we show how to augment the data with induced emotion and induced sentiment labels. we use our method to create and publish reactiongif , a first-of-its-kind affective dataset of 30k tweets. we provide baselines for three new tasks , including induced sentiment prediction and multilabel classification of induced emotions. our method and dataset open new research opportunities in emotion detection and affective computing.
