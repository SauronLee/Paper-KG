Annotating Online Misogyny | Philine Zeinert | online misogyny , a category of online abusive language , has serious and harmful social consequences. automatic detection of misogynistic language online , while imperative , poses complicated challenges to both data gathering , data annotation , and bias mitigation , as this type of data is linguistically complex and diverse. this paper makes three contributions in this area: firstly , we describe the detailed design of our iterative annotation process and codebook. secondly , we present a comprehensive taxonomy of labels for annotating misogyny in natural written language , and finally , we introduce a high-quality dataset of annotated posts sampled from social media posts.
