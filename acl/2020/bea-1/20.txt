A Comparative Study of Synthetic Data Generation Methods for Grammatical Error Correction | Max White | grammatical error correction \( gec \) is concerned with correcting grammatical errors in written text. current gec systems , namely those leveraging statistical and neural machine translation , require large quantities of annotated training data , which can be expensive or impractical to obtain. this research compares techniques for generating synthetic data utilized by the two highest scoring submissions to the restricted and low-resource tracks in the bea-2019 shared task on grammatical error correction.
