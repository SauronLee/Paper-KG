Give Me Convenience and Give Her Death: Who Should Decide What Uses of NLP are Appropriate, and on What Basis? | Kobi Leins | as part of growing nlp capabilities , coupled with an awareness of the ethical dimensions of research , questions have been raised about whether particular datasets and tasks should be deemed off-limits for nlp research. we examine this question with respect to a paper on automatic legal sentencing from emnlp 2019 which was a source of some debate , in asking whether the paper should have been allowed to be published , who should have been charged with making such a decision , and on what basis. we focus in particular on the role of data statements in ethically assessing research , but also discuss the topic of dual use , and examine the outcomes of similar debates in other scientific disciplines.
